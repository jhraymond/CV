Milo Manara's History of Humanity

Milo Manara's illustrations of human history through the ages is a phenomenal peice of work. There is a total of 23 panels that span human history from Paleolithic Era times up to the Modern Era. Each panel itself seems to span a few centuries and displays societal relationships between genders and classes by using individuals from various cultures. Each panel also shows the succession of predominance by a culture group as well. There is a 24th panel depicts a question mark, which implies the uncertainty of the future.

Paleolithic - Mesolithic

The Paleolithic Period is an ancient cultural stage of human development, characterized by the use of rudimentary chipped stone tools as found in the archeological record. This period also saw the rise of Homo sapiens, bipedalism, the control of fire and worldwide migrations out of Africa.

Institution of "Marriage" and the Family

Further development of the most basic societal unit: the family. A group of related individuals either through blood or meaningful relationships.

Neolithic - Chalcolithic - Eneolithic

The newer stone age of human development characterized by larger societies, refined stone tools, and the beginnings of agriculture and domestication of animals. This image also represents the transition from Neolithic to Chalcolithic, or Copper Age, where people began using fire to manipulate materials. Among those would be ceramics and copper smelting. Lastly, the image shows the early Bronze Age organization of nascent empires.

Egyptian and Babylonian

With the rise of iron tool and weapon use, came the rise of empires across the Fertile Crescent. The image illustrates the presence of the Egyptian and Babylonian Empires, respectively.

Assyrian, Mycenaean, Achaemenids

This image illustrates the Assyrians, Mycenaeans and Achaemenids rise to the world stage as powers.

Hellenic - Ancient to Classical

This image illustrates the rise of the Hellenic kingdoms and democratic city-states, their Spartan wars, and the Athenian penchant for pedophilia.

Roman Period

On comes the Roman Empire. Through its Senate, gladiatorial spectacles, orgies, and domination of "lesser" cultures, conquered much of the known world.

Germanic Migrations

After the Fall of the Western Roman Empire, the Dark Ages, those who had been conquered adopted much of the culture the Romans. They used what they learned to advance their own societies.

Byzantine Era

The Eastern Roman Empire, having survived the Dark Ages, allowed Christianity to flourish in its realm. Still Roman in organization, it took on Greek flavors of culture.

Feudalism

With rise of European nobles came the rise of feudalism, where rulers doled out privilege to those who swore fealty to them. Serfs lived and died by the Lord's will.

Crusader Era

After the rise of Islam, Muslims conquered much of the for Roman and Byzantine provinces, thus necessitating the need to reclaim the Holy Place in the Levant, resulting in the Crusades.

Medieval

This image illustrates landed and entrenched royalty, wars amongst those in power helped determine who would expand their territories. Religious differences in doctrine precipitated a reason for these wars, both domestic and foreign.

Renaissance

After grinding it out in the Middle Ages, there was a renewed interest in the knowledge of the Ancient Civilizations and their contributions to knowledge. Among those, as preserved, translated and advanced by Muslim scholars during Europe's Dark Ages, were Greek theories on medicine, mathematics and philosophy, Hindu theories on astronomy and mathematics, and other disciplines preserved from the Latin.

Age of Exploration

With this new found knowledge, the Age of Discovery dawned. New trade routes were explored and brought travelers to new peoples. With them came disease, poorly interpreted religion, and war. The Conquistadors were amongst the most brutal as they crushed both the Aztec and Mayan Empires in the New World.

Colonialism

With these new lands thoroughly conquered and subjected, a period of colonialization started. Permanent settlements sprang up and provided the conquerors with new means of revenue. Those who ruled did so by Divine Right and whomsoever disagreed with that was subjected to Purification through Flame.

Enlightenment

The amount of stress put upon the people by their respective rulers, both church and state, precipitated independent and deductive reasoning based upon empirical knowledge and not tradition, which came to be viewed as superstitious and dogmatic. This new thought process led, in a few countries, to revolutions, namely the American and French.

Napoleonic - Victorian Era

After the social revolutions, men, not of noble birth, but no less capable, came to the fore to lead their respective societies. In America, there came the Founding Fathers who drafted the US Constitution, in France their came the Directory and the Consulate. After the defeat of Napoleon, Victorian England expanded its influence across the world.

Manifest Destiny

After the solid establishment of the 13 colonies and the US civil war, the US government under James Madison invoked the idea of "Manifest Destiny". The idea that the US was destined to expand across North America at the expense of Native Americans already living there.

European Imperialism 1

With European colonialism spread worldwide, came European Imperialism.
This oppressive system touched the Inuit, North Africans, Sub-Saharan Africans, Masai, Khoi Khoi, Austronesians, Boers, Amazonian Indians, Polynesians...

European Imperialism 2

...Egpytians, Ottomans, Cossacks, Arabs, Persians, Indians, Chinese and Japanese peoples.

The Great War

This worldwide imperialism was subject to policies made by the crown, including war. When two imperial countries went to war, they drug numerous other peoples into the conflict as well. Thus we had the Great War.

World War II

In the aftermath of the Great War, new governments came to be, namely the Ottoman became the Turk, the Russian became a republic. Yet still there were wounds in the hearts of men who sought recompense for their countries' defeat. Thus ignited World War II.

Cold War Era

With the end of the Second World War, new world powers arose and kept the other in check, the United States and the Soviet Union. While engaging with each other through spycraft, diplomacy, and client proxy wars, the two superpowers never came into direct conflict.

Next...?

When the Soviet Union ended, the Cold War era ended with it. This left the US as the sole superpower in the world. US hegemony could now act unilaterally anywhere in the world largely unopposed.
